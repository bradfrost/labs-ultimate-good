What Actually Works Rapid Grid Decarbonization

Solar and wind are now the cheapest energy sources ever created. The technology exists to decarbonize electricity generation within two decades. The barrier is political will, not technical capability.
Electrify Everything

Transport, heating, and industrial processes can be electrified and powered by clean grids. Electric vehicles are already at cost parity with combustion engines in many markets.
Methane Reduction

Methane has 80x the warming potential of CO2 over 20 years. Cutting methane from agriculture and fossil fuel leaks is the fastest way to slow warming.
Carbon Pricing

Putting a price on carbon emissions and removing fossil fuel subsidies ($7 trillion globally) creates the economic incentives for the transition.
Reforestation & Land Use

Nature-based solutions can absorb significant carbon while restoring ecosystems. Not a silver bullet, but a critical complement to emissions cuts.
How to Actually Do It
  1. Use Project Drawdown's "Table of Solutions" as your primary reference
  2. Support clean energy legislation and grid modernization
  3. Advocate for fossil fuel subsidy removal
  4. Reduce personal emissions — but focus most energy on systemic change
  5. Vote for climate-literate candidates at every level of government
 What Digital Makers Can Do

You design and build digital things. Here's how those skills apply.

 Individual

Measure and reduce the carbon footprint of the digital products you build. Optimize images, reduce JavaScript bloat, choose green hosting. A lighter website is a greener website. Practice sustainable web design as a discipline, not an afterthought.

 Community

Build tools for local climate action groups — carbon calculators, ride-share coordinators, community solar sign-up flows. Help local governments create accessible public transit apps that make it easier to leave the car at home.

 Country

Design data visualizations that make climate data visceral and local, not abstract and global. Build tools that help citizens track their representatives' climate voting records and fossil fuel industry donations.

 World

Contribute to open-source climate monitoring platforms like Climate Trace. Build interfaces for Project Drawdown's solutions data that make the path forward tangible. Design for the Global South — the communities hit first and hardest need the best tools, not the worst.
